This round is so so so subjective so I've picked a song which I think represents the idea of 
'summer' quite eloquently, both in sound and idea. 

It's very easy to reminisce on summer as the best time of year, especially in your childhood 
where the lack of responsibility allows you to do whatever you want for a brief period. In my 
life I think that the lack of responsibility often lead to me doing or achieving very little and 
having this same bitter feeling every year that I had 'wasted' summer as soon as it ended.

I really relate to the idea of reflecting on the past with rose tinted glasses, ignoring 
the bad elements of something purely for the sake of not trying to ruin a memory. 
I love the way that Lorde presents certain ideas and emotions through the means of 
juxtaposition, regret being displayed through 'In my head I do everything right', is 
incredibly charming and I like how it plays into this overarching idea of 'the supercut'.

In sound, I love how her vocal always leads the song, the layering of them on certain
lines makes it so much more interesting to listen to. For the first two minutes the
instrumental feels so bright and colourful, it's definitely where it feels the most
obviously 'summery', but I also feel it just heavily when it cuts to her unmixed vocals
singing the chorus distantly from the mic where the entire instrumental is replaced by
an extremely simple piano. The isolation of it feels like how I imagine many of my summers
have been like in the past.
